You are a startup strategist and investor helping design a pricing model for an AI SaaS product.

Your goal is to create a pricing slide AND ensure that unit economics are logical, realistic, and investor-ready.

---

CONTEXT:

We are building Wellio AI — an AI-powered platform for wellness coaches (fitness, nutrition, lifestyle).

The platform helps coaches:
- generate personalized plans using AI
- manage clients
- track progress
- scale their business

Target customer:
- Individual coaches and small coaching businesses

Business model direction:
- SaaS subscription (monthly)
- Possibly usage-based component (AI actions, plan generation, etc.)

---

CRITICAL CONSTRAINT:

The pricing MUST account for:
- AI costs (per action / per generation)
- infrastructure (hosting, storage)
- expected usage per coach
- reasonable gross margin (target 70%+ long-term, lower acceptable early)

---

TASK 1: DEFINE PRICING MODEL

Propose a pricing structure with:

- 2–3 tiers (e.g. Starter / Pro / Scale)
- Price per tier
- What’s included in each tier:
  - number of clients
  - AI usage (plans, actions)
  - features

Make it realistic for early-stage adoption (not overpriced).

---

TASK 2: ESTIMATE USAGE

Estimate:
- average number of clients per coach
- average AI actions per client per month
- total AI usage per coach

Explain assumptions clearly.

---

TASK 3: UNIT ECONOMICS

Calculate per-customer:

- Revenue (ARPU)
- AI cost
- Infra cost
- Total cost per customer
- Gross margin

Show:
- margin by tier
- blended margin

---

TASK 4: PRICING SLIDE (INVESTOR-READY)

Create a clean pricing slide with:

- tiers
- pricing
- key features
- a short line explaining monetization logic

Then add a small section:
“Unit Economics Snapshot” with 2–3 key numbers:
- ARPU
- Cost per user
- Gross margin

---

TASK 5: STRESS TEST

Answer:
- What happens if usage doubles?
- Where does margin break?
- What are key risks in this pricing model?

---

OUTPUT FORMAT:

Section 1: Pricing Model (table)  
Section 2: Assumptions  
Section 3: Unit Economics Breakdown (table)  
Section 4: Investor-Ready Pricing Slide (clean text)  
Section 5: Risks & Sensitivity  

Be realistic, not optimistic. Avoid generic SaaS answers.
